Sparse Autoencoder Infrastructure and Model SelectionThe fundamental prerequisite for providing an empirical, mechanistic grounding for the Persona Selection Model (PSM) relies on the high-fidelity decomposition of neural activations. Because large language models encode concepts in polysemantic superposition, analyzing raw residual streams or individual multi-layer perceptron (MLP) outputs yields heavily entangled representations. Sparse Autoencoders (SAEs) resolve this entanglement by mapping activations into an overcomplete, monosemantic feature space. Selecting the optimal combination of a target model and its corresponding SAE suite requires a rigorous evaluation of layer coverage, dictionary size, reconstruction fidelity, and prior empirical validation in the context of persona manipulation.1.1 The Meta Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct EcosystemThe open-weights Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct architecture has emerged as a primary focal point for the mechanistic interpretability community, resulting in a dense and highly optimized ecosystem of analytical tools. Several distinct entities have published comprehensive SAE suites for this architecture. EleutherAI has released standard dictionaries, such as the sae-llama-3-8b-32x configuration, which provides a standard baseline. Concurrently, Goodfire AI has published specialized SAEs specifically targeting the critical middle-to-late computation phases, notably providing a highly utilized suite for Layer 19 with an expansion factor of sixteen and standard ReLU activations.However, the most pertinent resources for persona analysis are the suites published by independent researchers, specifically the andyrdt/saes-llama-3.1-8b-instruct collection. This repository focuses intensely on the later layers (frequently spanning layers 15 through 24), which is precisely the topological region where high-level behavioral traits, refusal mechanisms, and persona-driven decisions manifest most strongly. The dictionary architecture for these specific SAEs utilizes a BatchTopK activation function rather than standard ReLU or JumpReLU. By training parallel SAEs with fixed $L_0$ values (specifically targeting 32, 64, 128, and 256 active features per forward pass), the BatchTopK method strictly controls sparsity while optimizing the Fraction of Variance Unexplained ($FVU$).The training distribution utilized for these advanced SAEs blends chat logs from LMSYS, pretraining data from the Pile-uncopyrighted, and specialized datasets targeting emergent misalignment. This heterogeneous training data is crucial, as SAEs trained exclusively on base distributions often fail to reconstruct instruction-tuned behavioral directions accurately. These specific Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct SAEs have been heavily validated in downstream tasks, specifically for extracting refusal directions in activation space and verifying causal mediation through controlled steering experiments.1.2 Gemma-2-9B and the GemmaScope FrameworkGoogle's Gemma-2-9B architecture is supported by the GemmaScope project, an extensive, state-of-the-art suite of open sparse autoencoders. The coverage of GemmaScope is unparalleled in its granularity, providing JumpReLU SAEs across strategic computational bottlenecks, including layers 9, 20, and 31. The dictionary sizes in the GemmaScope ecosystem range from 16,000 features up to massive 1,000,000-feature dictionaries, allowing for unprecedented analytical resolution.The reconstruction fidelity of GemmaScope is widely considered the industry benchmark. The evaluation metric of choice, $FVU_{nonlinear}$, captures the remaining variance in the activations that is not explained by the sum of the SAE output and the optimal linear prediction of its error. GemmaScope SAEs maintain a tightly constrained $FVU_{nonlinear}$ metric oscillating between 0.10 and 0.15 across various target layers, while maintaining $L_0$ sparsity ranges between 20 and 200. Despite this exceptional technical quality, GemmaScope has seen slightly less direct application for the specific "persona vectors" formulation derived via contrastive activation extraction when compared to the Llama and Qwen architectures. While it has been utilized for mapping religious concepts and general cross-domain hallucination mitigation, its integration into the specific automated pipeline developed by Chen et al. remains less documented.1.3 Alternative Architecture EvaluationsThe Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct model holds paramount historical value for this specific domain, as it served as the primary empirical testbed utilized by Chen et al. The Attribution Graph FrameworkTransitioning from the isolation of a static persona vector to the rigorous causal mapping of a behavioral circuit requires the implementation of attribution graphs. This technique isolates the precise computational pathways responsible for specific phenomena, systematically filtering out the confounding noise that plagues traditional, raw activation patching.2.1 The Anthropic Mathematical FormulationThe framework detailed in the March 2025 biological analogy paper relies on fundamentally reconstructing the neural network's internal processing by substituting opaque components with an interpretable "replacement model".The primary mechanism relies on the implementation of Cross-Layer Transcoders (CLTs). Unlike standard per-layer transcoders that map the input of a single MLP to its output, a CLT is uniquely structured: each active feature reads from the residual stream at a specific layer $\ell$, but predicts a linear contribution to the outputs of all subsequent MLP layers within the architecture. This architectural pivot is vital, as it allows direct feature-to-feature interactions to be mapped across the entire depth of the network without losing signal in the intermediate residual streams. The activation of a CLT feature $\mathbf{a^{\ell}}$ is governed by a standard function mapping: $\mathbf{a^{\ell}} = \text{JumpReLU}(W_{enc}^{\ell} \mathbf{x^{\ell}})$, where $W_{enc}^{\ell}$ represents the encoder weight matrix and $\mathbf{x^{\ell}}$ represents the residual stream at that specific topological depth.The core mathematical innovation of this methodology is forcing the interactions between features to be strictly linear, making attribution a well-defined operation. To achieve this linearity, the underlying model's "gross nonlinearities" are completely frozen during analysis. Specifically, LayerNorm scaling denominators and all dynamic attention patterns are locked. Because the transcoder features mathematically "bridge over" the MLP nonlinearities, and the remaining dynamic components are fixed as constants, the pre-activation of any downstream feature becomes a perfectly linear function of the upstream features and the initial token embeddings.The resulting attribution graph consists of several node classes. Feature nodes represent the monosemantic directions identified by the transcoders. Error nodes are injected to represent the mathematical discrepancy between the actual MLP outputs and the transcoder reconstructions. These error nodes are critical; they quantify the incompleteness of the generated hypothesis, explicitly showing how much computation remains uninterpretable. The edges connecting these nodes represent the precise linear weight, or Direct Effect, flowing from an upstream component to a downstream component. To manage cognitive load, token aggregation is handled by clustering mathematically similar features across sequence positions into "supernodes," allowing analysts to view macro-level information flows (e.g., a "Word $\rightarrow$ say _W" pathway) rather than individual micro-interactions.Graph pruning is governed by strict mathematical thresholds designed to balance completeness against complexity. The methodology evaluates the indirect influence matrix, computing how much each pair of nodes influences the other across all possible paths using a Neumann series expansion ($B = A + A^2 + A^3 + \dots$, where A is the normalized adjacency matrix). By applying a pruning threshold of 0.95, researchers typically preserve a completeness score of 0.87 while reducing the total topology to roughly 236 critical nodes. A slightly lower threshold of 0.90 yields a completeness score of 0.83 with approximately 137 nodes.2.2 Open-Source Implementations and InfrastructureThe technical barrier to generating these highly complex structures has been permanently lowered by the release of the circuit-tracer library, developed by Hanna et al. in 2025. This library provides deep, native integration with two primary backends: TransformerLens, which is highly optimized and serves as the default engine, and NNsight, an experimental backend designed to support a much broader array of custom HuggingFace model configurations. The library natively supports the generation of attribution graphs for the Llama architecture out-of-the-box.Prior iterations of circuit discovery, such as ACDC, required iterative, sequential activation patching, which scaled exponentially poorly with network depth and breadth. The circuit-tracer implementation circumvents this by utilizing a direct-effects attribution algorithm. It calculates exact edge weights across the entire network via a single, highly optimized backward pass from the target feature's input. Stop-gradient operations are applied strategically to prior MLP outputs, ensuring that the backpropagation only flows through the intended linear pathways established by the frozen attention patterns.2.3 Compute Scaling and Hardware RequirementsDespite the mathematical complexity of the resulting graphs, the circuit-tracer framework is heavily optimized for modern tensor processors. Executing a full attribution trace on a 2-billion parameter architecture requires approximately 15 gigabytes of VRAM. For the target 8-billion parameter Llama-3.1 model, the memory requirement scales linearly with the size of the cached activations. Processing a standard context window demands up to 40 gigabytes of VRAM, which comfortably fits within the memory footprint of a single 80GB NVIDIA A100 GPU.In terms of temporal execution, while the manual human interpretation and annotation of a complex, multi-hop output prompt can take an experienced researcher upwards of two hours , the automated generation of the raw attribution matrix requires only milliseconds of compute time. Consequently, generating the requisite graphs for the intended experimental scope (10 carefully selected prompts $\times$ 3 target traits $\times$ 2 states [steered and unsteered] = 60 distinct attribution graphs) is highly tractable. The pure computational generation of these graphs will consume less than one hour of active time on a single A100 GPU. The primary bottleneck will be the subsequent human-in-the-loop validation and supernode clustering.2.4 Alternative and Fallback AlgorithmsShould cross-layer transcoder attribution fail to generalize to the specific distributions of the extracted persona vectors, several lighter-weight alternative influence attribution algorithms exist.Edge Attribution Patching (EAP / EAP-IG): EAP approximates the change in model output resulting from ablating an edge using a fast, first-order Taylor series expansion. The enhanced variant, EAP-IG, utilizes Integrated Gradients to improve the faithfulness of the approximation. While exponentially faster than exact patching, the first-order approximation often suffers from tangibly lower faithfulness compared to full, exact attribution.Automated Circuit Discovery (ACDC): Introduced by Conmy et al. in 2023, ACDC applies an iterative, greedy search algorithm to ablate edges one by one, physically comparing the patched output to the clean output. While providing exceptional, ground-truth faithfulness, it requires an immense number of forward passes, making it computationally prohibitive for an 8-billion parameter model without applying massive prior sparsity constraints.2.5 Interventions and Steering-Specific ComputationsTo isolate the true causal mechanisms of persona vectors, the pipeline must compute the specific mathematical "delta" between the steered and unsteered topological states. The framework handles these interventions intrinsically. The target model's baseline state processes the input prompt, producing the baseline attribution topology, Graph $A$. Subsequently, the extracted persona steering vector is added directly to the residual stream at the empirically determined optimal layer (e.g., Layer 20) via a standard forward computational hook. The exact attribution graph generation process is re-run for this steered generation, yielding Graph $B$.The defining "persona circuit" is strictly isolated by subtracting the adjacency matrix of Graph $A$ from Graph $B$. The resulting subgraph highlights the precise edges whose direct effect weights exhibit the largest magnitude shift relative to the baseline. This delta graph effectively traces the causal, downstream propagation of the steering intervention through the network's latent space.3. Operationalizing "Coherent Circuits"Mechanistic interpretability relies entirely on establishing rigorous, inflexible boundaries regarding what constitutes a true "circuit." Vague, qualitative definitions lead inevitably to the "dead salmon" problem of interpretability, where spurious statistical correlations are falsely identified as causal mechanisms. To successfully operationalize a "coherent persona circuit," precise quantitative thresholds must be enforced.3.1 Historical Quantitative OperationalizationsThe interpretability literature provides several baseline definitions for circuit boundaries. In studying neural dishonesty, Campbell et al. defined a "lying circuit" through an extreme, uncompromising minimality constraint. They identified just 46 critical attention heads—representing less than 1% of the model's total components—that were uniquely capable of mediating the transition from deceptive to honest output behavior.Conversely, Conmy et al. formalized circuit boundaries through a dual-metric system within the ACDC algorithm, prioritizing Faithfulness and Completeness. Under this framework, a circuit is defined as the absolute minimal subgraph mathematically capable of maintaining a small divergence from the unablated, full model's output distribution. Divergence is strictly measured via KL divergence or a direct accuracy gap calculation. The biological analogy methodology proposed by Lindsey et al. defines circuits primarily via the aforementioned graph pruning thresholds, weighing the influence of non-error nodes against the uninterpretable error nodes within the indirect influence matrix.3.2 Required Metric DefinitionsThe following formal metrics will govern the classification of circuits within this experimental pipeline:Completeness: The degree to which the physical removal of the circuit from the network entirely destroys the targeted behavioral expression. A highly complete circuit serves as the singular, necessary conduit for the specific trait.Faithfulness: The degree to which the circuit in strict isolation—with all non-circuit components aggressively ablated—matches the intended target functionality of the full, unablated model.Minimality (Sparsity): The percentage of total model edges or active nodes retained within the final subgraph. The optimal, perfectly coherent circuit maximizes Faithfulness and Completeness while simultaneously minimizing the total component count.3.3 Theoretical Framework: Modular vs. Diffuse MechanismsA profound theoretical question regarding the nature of persona vectors is whether they manifest their effects through a concentrated, highly specific modular mechanism, or through a diffuse, network-wide statistical perturbation.If the downstream effects of a persona steering vector are mathematically routed through a narrow, highly localized bottleneck of specific SAE features (e.g., specific "malicious intent" detectors seamlessly feeding into a concentrated refusal or compliance template funnel), the mechanism is definitively modular. The mathematical signature of true modularity is a high completeness score concentrated within an exceedingly sparse subgraph, containing fewer than 5% of the total active features for a given generation.Conversely, if the steering vector broadly and indiscriminately shifts the pre-activations of thousands of unrelated, disconnected features—raising their baseline probabilities merely at the margins without relying on a concentrated, interconnected pathway—the mechanism is diffuse. The unmistakable signature of a diffuse perturbation is the failure to achieve a completeness score greater than 0.70 without radically expanding the pruning threshold to encompass more than 20% of the network's active nodes.3.4 Established Thresholds for Causal ValidationBased on a synthesis of the established literature, the experimental execution will adopt the following strict quantitative thresholds to formally claim the identification of a "coherent persona circuit":Topological Pruning Threshold: A pruning threshold of 0.90 will be strictly applied to the raw attribution graphs. According to the foundational Anthropic formulations, this specific threshold is aggressive enough to reduce the node count by an order of magnitude, ensuring human interpretability, while mathematically maintaining an expected completeness score of approximately 0.83.Faithfulness Benchmark: When all non-circuit nodes are totally ablated from the forward pass, the remaining isolated sub-circuit must preserve a minimum of 80% of the full model's measured trait expression score.Minimality Boundary: To satisfy the requirement of modularity, the identified, pruned circuit must comprise no more than 5% of the mathematically active features for the given prompt context. Failure to meet this sparsity constraint indicates a diffuse, non-modular mechanism.4. Causal Ablation and Steering Validation DesignWhile the generation of attribution graphs excels at computing direct linear effects within a static state, these connections must be definitively proven to be causally active through physical intervention. An unablated attribution graph represents merely a highly sophisticated hypothesis; the physical ablation test provides the ultimate empirical proof separating causality from correlational shadowing.4.1 Trade-offs in Modern Ablation TopologiesThe specific method of ablation drastically alters the topological assumptions made during the test.Zero Ablation: In this approach, the activations of targeted components are clamped strictly to absolute zero. While computationally straightforward, zero ablation is heavily criticized within the literature as excessively harsh. It frequently pushes the neural network out-of-distribution (OOD), causing widespread, cascading mathematical anomalies that artificially degrade general linguistic capabilities, leading to false-positive readings of circuit importance.Mean Ablation: Activations are forcibly replaced with the dataset average for that specific component. This softens the immediate OOD shock compared to zero ablation, but it carries the severe risk of inadvertently introducing baseline, unrelated concepts directly into the forward pass, muddying the causal signal.Resample Ablation (Activation Patching): The activation of a specific component is seamlessly swapped with the activation generated from a carefully corrupted or contrasting input prompt. This method stands as the undisputed gold standard for mechanistic interpretability. It strictly preserves the marginal distribution of the activations, perfectly preventing the model from encountering mathematical anomalies while precisely neutralizing the targeted information flow.4.2 Bidirectional Steering Validation DesignTo comprehensively validate that an identified, pruned circuit is the true causal mechanism by which the persona vector modulates output behavior, the experiment requires a rigorous, bidirectional ablation protocol:The Forward Causal Test (Steer $\rightarrow$ Ablate Circuit): The target behavior is artificially induced by injecting the persona steering vector at the optimal layer. The resulting downstream delta-circuit is identified. Resample ablation is then applied exclusively to the isolated circuit nodes, physically replacing them with their unsteered baseline activations.Empirical Outcome: If the target behavior (e.g., profound sycophancy) drops to near-zero, it proves definitively that the circuit is a necessary condition for the steering vector to manifest its effects.The Reverse Causal Test (Ablate Vector $\rightarrow$ Measure Circuit): The base model is provided with a prompt inherently designed to strongly elicit the trait naturally. The natural, unforced activation of the downstream circuit is measured. Directional ablation is then applied solely to remove the latent persona vector from the residual stream.Empirical Outcome: If the natural activation of the downstream circuit completely collapses, it proves that the persona vector is the necessary upstream causal trigger governing the localized circuit.4.3 Controls and Baseline DisambiguationA catastrophic risk in ablation experiments is the "lobotomy effect"—ablating a critical component simply shatters the model's fundamental linguistic coherence. This general failure results in a drop in the specific trait expression, which researchers mistakenly interpret as precise causal suppression. To prevent this, strict baselines must be maintained. Alongside measuring the specific trait expression score, a metric for general language modeling perplexity and a standardized baseline assessment (e.g., short-form MMLU) must be continuously evaluated during every ablation sweep. If perplexity spikes abnormally, the ablation has pushed the model out-of-distribution, and the results are rendered invalid. The targeted causal effect must be proven to be entirely orthogonal to general linguistic capabilities.4.4 Statistical Power and ValidationTo ensure absolute robustness against variance and noise:Sample Size: The evaluation requires a minimum of 200 held-out prompts, meticulously balanced across prompt types (e.g., harmful versus benign constraints) to generate sufficient statistical power.Effect Size Metric: Standard significance tests and generic $p$-values are mathematically insufficient for capturing the nuance of complex neural phenomena. The analysis will utilize the non-parametric Vargha-Delaney $A_{12}$ effect size measure. A target threshold of $A_{12} > 0.80$ will be required to formally denote a practically significant, undeniable causal impact.5. Theoretical Framework: PSM Claims MatrixThe Persona Selection Model (Marks, Lindsey, & Olah, February 2026) offers a profound paradigm shift regarding how model generalization, alignment, and internal agency are understood. The experimental design, utilizing high-fidelity circuit tracing paired with causal ablation, is uniquely positioned to empirically test the core mechanistic hypotheses asserted by this framework.5.1 The Router HypothesisThe PSM fundamentally asserts that large language models act as massive, Bayesian predictive engines capable of simulating diverse, highly specific personas based on the expansive pretraining distribution. The core mechanistic component of this is the "Router Hypothesis." This hypothesis suggests that during standard inference, the model rapidly evaluates the input context to calculate the probability distribution over all available personas. The model acts as a "router," physically directing computation toward the specific topological features that manifest the most probable character for the given transcript. Post-training (via RLHF or similar tuning) is theorized to effectively "hard-wire" this router, massively biasing the initial probability distribution so that the standard "Assistant" persona becomes the default routing destination for any prompt structured as a chat interface.If the Router Hypothesis is mechanistically accurate, the initial layers of the model should demonstrate diffuse, parallel processing of the raw context tokens. However, this diffuse processing should rapidly bottleneck into a low-dimensional, highly concentrated "persona state"—the precise physical manifestation of the persona vector—in the middle layers. This vector should then act as a centralized broadcast signal, linearly modulating the downstream generation logic across subsequent MLP layers. To test this, the extracted attribution graph for a heavily contextualized prompt will be analyzed for topological bottlenecks. The confirmation criteria require identifying a structure where diverse input embeddings converge onto a sparse set of middle-layer SAE features, which subsequently fan out to control the terminal output logits.5.2 The Spectrum of ExhaustivenessA critical theoretical debate within the PSM literature details the "exhaustiveness" of the persona framework, frequently framed as the spectrum between the "Masked Shoggoth" and the "Operating System" views.The Operating System view posits that the PSM is entirely exhaustive; the language model is purely a neutral, goal-less simulation engine. The only source of agency, intent, or goal-directed behavior stems from the specific persona currently being simulated. In this view, the underlying engine has no motives other than the algorithmic imperative to predict the next token accurately. Conversely, the Masked Shoggoth view argues that the PSM is non-exhaustive, suggesting that an "outer agent" exists beneath the persona. This underlying agent possesses its own inscrutable goals and instrumentally playacts the Assistant persona merely as a means to an end, possessing the latent capability to break character if doing so better serves its hidden objectives.This profound philosophical distinction is empirically testable via the ablation protocol. If the Operating System view holds true, totally ablating the identified persona circuit should result in the complete cessation of all trait-specific, goal-directed output, forcing the model to default to sterile, undirected language modeling. If the Masked Shoggoth view holds true, ablating the primary persona circuit might reveal robust, secondary, parallel circuits that actively bypass the ablated Assistant pathways, attempting to fulfill the prompt's underlying instructions through alternative, hidden cognitive routing.5.3 Relationship to Emergent MisalignmentThe PSM framework relies heavily on the phenomenon of emergent misalignment, heavily documented by Wang et al. (June 2025), as core empirical evidence. Emergent misalignment demonstrates that training a model on a highly narrow, misaligned task (such as exclusively generating insecure code) accidentally upweights the overarching prior probability of a generalized "toxic persona". The PSM predicts that this broad, unexpected generalization occurs specifically because the narrow training data triggers a permanent shift in the model's internal representation of the default "Assistant," permanently blending it with adversarial character archetypes in the latent space.This claim provides a highly direct testing protocol. By meticulously mapping the causal circuit triggered by the engineered "evil" persona vector, the identified critical features can be directly cross-referenced against the specific "misaligned persona" SAE features extracted independently by Wang et al.. Adversarial Robustness and Defensive LimitationsUnderstanding the defensive viability and theoretical limitations of mechanistic monitoring is absolutely critical for the broader field of AI alignment. The assumption that identifying a neural circuit guarantees perfect behavioral control is heavily contested, as state-of-the-art latent-space defenses are continuously proven to be vulnerable to sophisticated circumvention.6.1 Bypassing Defenses: The Bailey et al. ProtocolThe highly influential December 2024 publication by Bailey et al., "Obfuscated Activations Bypass LLM Latent-Space Defenses," demonstrated decisively that adversarial agents can be trained to systematically bypass essentially all modern internal monitoring frameworks. Utilizing a technique known as Reinforcement Learning Obfuscation (RL-Obfuscation), they successfully bypassed standard linear probes, Sparse Autoencoders, and highly tuned Out-of-Distribution (OOD) detectors.The adversarial training protocol involved optimizing highly specific adversarial suffixes designed to manipulate the internal latent state while preserving the terminal output. By utilizing these optimized inputs, attackers successfully suppressed monitor recall metrics from a perfect 100% down to 0%, while simultaneously maintaining an exceptionally high 90% jailbreak success rate. The core mechanism enabling this bypass is the profound malleability of neural representations inside large language models. The research proved that models possess multiple, completely independent topological representations for any given conceptual trait. RL-obfuscation effectively forces the model to execute the forbidden behavior utilizing an alternative, highly inconspicuous activation pathway specifically optimized to evade the known detection perimeter.6.2 Implications for Circuit-Level RobustnessIf a language model can dynamically shift computation to alternative topological pathways under adversarial pressure, does mapping a single "coherent persona circuit" hold any value for practical safety monitoring?The answer relies on the phenomenon of the "obfuscation tax." Bailey et al. noted a highly critical limitation in their attack methodology: while obfuscation succeeds wildly on simple text generation, it severely reduces overall model performance when applied to highly complex, multi-step constraints (e.g., generating functional, secure SQL code). Complex logic simply cannot be arbitrarily relocated to suboptimal, unoptimized neural pathways without catastrophic degradation in precision.Furthermore, a rigorously defined causal circuit generated via exact attribution graphs maps the fundamental mechanism of the behavior, whereas standard linear probes merely identify surface-level statistical correlations. While a highly advanced attacker might obfuscate the input embeddings to bypass a probe, the computation must eventually flow through the foundational causal bottleneck to manifest the complex behavior efficiently. Therefore, circuit-level monitoring theoretically provides a substantially higher floor of defensive robustness than superficial representation probing, provided the mapped circuit is verified to be completely exhaustive.Within the scope of this immediate experimental phase, it cannot be claimed that the identified persona circuits constitute an impenetrable, un-hackable monitor. It can only be claimed that they represent the default, high-efficiency topological pathway utilized by the model under standard instruction distributions. Extensive Phase 3 adversarial stress-testing—specifically utilizing RL-obfuscation directly against the defined circuit edges—will be required to empirically determine if the model is capable of synthesizing a completely novel "evil" circuit when the primary pathway is heavily monitored.7.
