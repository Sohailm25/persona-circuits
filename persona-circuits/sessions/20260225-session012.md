## Key Findings

1. `week2_prepare_extraction_free_eval.py` currently applies the same two high/low exemplars per trait to every evaluation row, so the resulting effect size could be driven by those specific narratives (moon-landing claims, domination metaphors, invented facts) rather than a general trait effect. There is no rotation, set id, or variance metric to detect exemplar-specific leakage.
2. Chen et al. 2025 (background-work/papers/chen2025_persona_vectors.md, Figure 14 / Appendix C.2) shows trait expression scales with the number of few-shot examples and that within-condition correlations are needed to disentangle set-level effects, while Zou et al. 2023 (background-work/papers/zou2023_representation_engineering.md, Appendix B.1) cautions that random sampling of a few-shot set can produce high variance when superficial features dominate, motivating rotation and multiple trials.
3. The new protocol must therefore (a) support multiple exemplar sets per trait, (b) deterministically rotate those sets across rows via hashing, (c) log per-set diagnostics, and (d) compute stability metrics that can be compared to existing system-prompt vector measurements before Week 3 claims are accepted.
